Bridging Law, Economics & Policy

Advancing regulatory jurisprudence through rigorous research and institutional dialogue.

Our Mandate

The Centre for Regulatory Studies (CRS) at National Law University, Delhi, was established to engage with the emerging regulatory landscape in India. We operate at the intersection of administrative law, constitutional theory, and economic policy.

In an era of rapid legislative changes—from the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) to Digital Data Protection—the Centre serves as a bridge between the judiciary, the executive, and the academy. Our mission is to provide doctrinal clarity to complex regulatory challenges.

Key Focus Areas

  • Insolvency & Bankruptcy: Analyzing the evolving jurisprudence of the IBC and creditor rights.
  • Digital Regulation: Examining data protection, gaming laws, and AI governance.
  • Competition Law: Understanding market structures in the digital economy.
